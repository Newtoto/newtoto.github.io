Shield Man (January 2018 - Current) - Team Project

Description

Shield Man is a hardcore 2D action platformer inspired by games such as super meat boy, with level time trials and humorous aspects. It is made in Unity.

You play as a knight who is on a quest to retrieve his princess, who also happens to be his sword. You navigate by bashing your shield against floors and walls instead of the more traditional running and jumping platformer mechanics.

This is the second game from Slender Loris after the team decided to step away from the development of Cookoo.


The team and my roles

This is the second game from Slender Loris after the team decided to step away from the development of Cookoo. Slender Loris consists of 10 students. 3 programmers (including myself), 2 artists, 3 designers, 1 audio engineer and 1 animator.

My main role on this project was gameplay programming, alongside elements of AI, save files, and menu navigation.

VR Rooftop Sniper (December 2017) - Solo Project

Description

This prototype was created for the Vive and made in Unity with SteamVR. An Arduino, sliding potentiometer and button was also used, along with a vive tracker and a custom spring mechanism in order to explore alternative forms of feedback outside of the standard vive controllers.

Progress pictures and videos are available in the Github repo, which is linked below.


Challenges

Since my access to tools and materials was limited, the gun model and firing mechanism was made out of recycled boxes and pringles tubes, a peg, bearings, allen keys, a spring, cable ties and a lot of duct tape. This meant that the strength was limited and I couldn't make the firing mechanism as powerful as I would have liked.

Trying to combine both electronic and mechanical parts made this considerably more difficult, because it meant that 1 button had to give a digital signal and fire the mechanism. As well as this, the reload had to load the mechanism and send over values to show its physical location.

Cookoo (September 2017 - Present) - Team Project

Description

Cookoo is currently an assymetrical couch co-op game in which one player drives, while the other player cooks in the back. This is made in Unity.

This game is still in progress and has plans to change dramatically over the course of the next year. These changes involve making the game a competitive game where both players cook in a fast paced mini-game style and then drive. The goal will be to grow your pancake business the most.


The team and my roles

The team for this game is made up of 10 students. 3 programmers (including myself), 2 artists, 3 designers, 1 audio engineer and 1 animator.

My main roles were programming the driving and the interactions between the driving and the chef. One of the other 2 programmers did the UI, and the other programmed the cooking.

Crossbeam (April 2017) - Solo Project

Description

Crossbeam is a 2D puzzle game made in UE4 where you shoot 4 turrets to around obstacles in order to hit targets.

This game is designed with a custom controller in mind which was made using a button, 2 potentiometers and an Arduino.


Challenges

I wanted the game to be fully controllable without the use of a keyboard and mouse, so I implemented a menu and level selection which could be navigated using this controller.

The main challenge with this project was that 4 potentiometers resulted in incomplete values being sent to unreal. After sanity checking the values coming in, there wasn't enough valid numbers being received to get a responsive 3rd and 4th turret. For this reason I ended up reducing the number to 2 which meant that I had to make the game work with fewer inputs.

Descension (March 2017) - Team Project

Description

Descension is a first person melee hoard survival game made in UE4.

It is set in the underworld and created by Demon Crypt. Fight your way through masses of disturbing creatures with a hammer or dual axes in order to unleash as much havoc as you can, before your inevitable death that is.


The team and my contribution

The Demon Crypt team consisted of 11 people, 3 programmers (including myself), 4 artists, 2 designers, 1 audio engineer and 1 animator. I was a dedicated programmer for this game, working on various aspects such as AI and a weapon switching system.

This was my first game project in UE4.

GGJ Hyper Heatwave Runner (January 2017) - Team Project

Description

Hyper Heatwave Runner is an endless runner game made in Unity for the global game jam 2017.

It is set from the perspective of a brit abroad in France trying to cool down in the heatwave by picking up chilling drinks and ice creams and avoiding hot chilli peppers. All this while a load of baguettes fly at your face.


The team and my contribution

The team of 7 for this project consisted of 3 programmers, 2 artists, and 2 designers.

I was involved with the programming, as well as making the music and sound effects for the game. This was my first time programming in Unity which was a challenge since the time frame for this game was 48 hours.

Global Game Jam Github repository

Squid Vs Eel (December 2016) - Team Project

Description

Squid VS Eel is a 2 player couch competitive game programmed in python using pygame.

It is played by sharing a single keyboard with each player taking on the role of either the squid or the eel, each possessing a set of unique abilities designed to inflict damage, or counter the opponent's attacks.

The use of cooldowns introduces an element of timing and skill.


The team and my contribution

The team consisted of 4 programmers, including myself.

For this game I was involved with both programming and design along with the 3 others, as well as making animations and procedurally generated music. This was my first Pygame project.
