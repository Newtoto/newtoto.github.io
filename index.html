Experience

Ecommerce Strategies Ltd (ECS) - Front End Web Developer

ECS is a small Magento specialist ecommerce web development company in affiliation with Vaimo. I worked there full time for around 1 year starting late 2015. The specific jobs that my role entailed and the skills I learned are outlined below.

Front-end web development: Using HTML, CSS, and PHP I edited Magento templates to realise the custom designs and specifications of the customer along with a team. As a result of this work, I am also familiar with grunt, gulp and less, as well as editing sites and pushing site changes using command line tools.

Formatting product databases: Using excel formulae on up to 40,000 unique SKUs at a time, I formatted product lists with their categories and information for the import into Magento using the "Magmi" mass product importer and Sequel Pro. This involved the import of both simple products and more complex configurable ones.

Writing blog posts: Over my time at ECS, I wrote around 20 blog posts about various topics surrounding ecommerce, such as SEO. Among these were posts about ECS's customers' site launches.

Customer training: When new customers needed to learn about how to operate their website, such as adding new products, categories, and pages, I would give them a training session. As a result, I have an in depth understanding of backend magento, and to a lesser extent, magento 2. I have also improved at teaching how to operate software in a simple to understand manner.

QA Testing: This involved thoroughly testing the websites on a number of different browsers and devices, looking for potential issues that customers might have.

Techademy volunteering

Techademy is a 2 day long session designed to teach primary and secondary school staff how to code using the raspberry pi and various other technologies such as the BBC micro and Makey Makey kit. The aim of this is that that they with then be able to pass on this knowledge onto their students and encourage more uptake and understanding in the field of computer programming.

As a volunteer, I assisted the teachers with any issues that they had while learning to code. On the second day, the attendees also undertook a tech-jam style project to make something. The teachers I was assisting with this were using the Makey Makey kit in order to make an educational game with a custom controller for which I guided the python programming for.

Portfolio

Squid Vs Eel

Description

Squid VS Eel is a 2 player couch competitive game programmed in python using pygame.

It is played by sharing a single keyboard with each player taking on the role of either the squid or the eel, each posessing a set of unique abilities designed to inflict damage, or counter the opponent's attacks.

The use of cooldowns introduces an element of timing and skill.

My role and contribution

The team consisted of 4 programmers, including myself.

For this game I was involved with both programming along with the 3 others, as well as making all the artwork and procedurally generated music used in the game.

Squid VS Eel Github repository

Hyper Heatwave Runner (Global Game Jam 2017)

Description

Hyper Heatwave Runner is an endless runner game set from the perspective of a brit abroad in France trying to cool down in the heatwave by picking up chilling drinks and ice creams and avoiding hot chilli peppers. All this while a load of baguettes fly at your face.

My role and contribution

The team of 7 for this project consisted of 3 programmers, 2 artists, and 2 designers.

I was involved with the programming, as well as making the music and sound effects for the game.
